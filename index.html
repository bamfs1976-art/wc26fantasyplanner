Captain doubles base xPts. Numbers reflect goal involvement probability scaled by ranking gap, plus clean sheet odds for defensive positions. The model is intentionally conservative. Standout performances (hat tricks, 10 plus point hauls) sit outside the median.

What the model does not capture

  • Injuries and rotation
  • Red cards or penalty misses
  • Hat tricks or 3 goal hauls (these pull the average up but are not predictable)
  • Scouting bonus (under 5% ownership scoring 4+) and direct free kick bonus
  • Live form going into the tournament

Captain

  • Captain scores double points
  • Vice captain takes the armband only if your captain plays zero minutes and you have not changed the team during the round
  • You can change captain manually until that player's match starts

Scoring highlights

  • Each position gets 1 more point per goal vs FPL
  • Clean sheet 5 points for GK and DEF (60 mins minimum)
  • Mid +1 per 3 tackles or per 2 chances created
  • Forwards +1 per 2 shots on target
  • No defensive contribution rewards (no DefCon)
  • Penalty involvement penalises miss or save regardless of outcome
  • Bonus: 1 extra point for direct free-kick goal
  • Differential bonus: 2 points if scoring 4+ and owned by under 5% of teams

Recommended booster plan

The strategy in one line

Save chips for moments when value is highest. Round 2 or 3 in groups, plus the knockouts. Avoid burning chips in round 1 unless you spot a clear edge.

Round 1: hold all chips

Use your unlimited pre-tournament transfers to nail your squad. Save Max Captain and 12th Man for later when fixtures sharpen.

Round 2: 12th Man on a Brazil player

Brazil v Haiti (gap 77) is the biggest mismatch of round 2. 12th Man lets Vinicius Jr count without taking a squad slot or budget. Strong shout if your squad lacks Brazilian flair.

Alternative: 12th Man on Harry Kane for England v Ghana (gap 70) if you already have Brazil exposure.

Round 3: Wildcard for full rebuild

Round 3 is when fixtures rotate hardest. Many round 1 and 2 favourites face their toughest game, and the heaviest gaps shift to Belgium v New Zealand (76), Morocco v Haiti (75), Croatia v Ghana (63), Argentina v Jordan (60).

Wildcard here lets you reload around these fixtures without taking transfer hits.

Round of 32: hold

Unlimited free transfers anyway. No need for chips. Save Qualification Booster for a round where most of your XI will progress.

Round of 16: Max Captain on a top scorer

By R16 the elite teams face stronger opposition. Max Captain guarantees your highest scorer gets the armband. Use when you have multiple strong captain options and cannot decide.

QF or SF: Qualification Booster

Use when your XI is loaded with players from teams you expect to progress. Each progressor gives +2 points. Strongest when you have 8+ likely progressors in your starting XI.

Mystery Booster: reactive

Revealed before R32. Hold and decide based on what it does.
